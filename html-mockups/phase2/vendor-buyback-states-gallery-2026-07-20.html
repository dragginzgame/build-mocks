First-pass mock for vendor buyback (V2 — removed from the V1 vendor spec), per Design Documentation/Phase2/vendor-buyback-design.md. A vendor may offer to buy a newly claimed token back from the purchaser within a window after the claim, through the shared offer system. Proposed gating: a Reputation-gated Project Mastery unlock (Economy branch — threshold TBD). Configured per vendor (vendorFeePercentage) — never part of a revenue preset.

01Vendor creation · buyback configurationVendor-level settings: on/off, the window (3 minutes – 1 week after each claim), and the margin (0–20%). The fee breakdown makes the guarantee visible: beneficiary costs and the Toko share of the original claim are always recovered first, so a buyback never costs the project money; the margin is the only creator-set variable (0% = buy back at cost).

03Go-Live caution · buyback + no scheduled endProceeds are held in the vendor's subaccount and release only after the vendor ends and every buyback window has expired. A vendor with no scheduled end would lock funds until manually stopped — so this combination gets the amber text-heavy caution (warns, never blocks). Every vendor always retains a way to be stopped.

This vendor has buyback on and no scheduled end. Claim proceeds stay locked in the vendor's account until you stop it yourself and the last 3-day buyback window has expired. 05After the claim · the offer in the holder's walletThe buyback runs through the standard offer system: the holder sees a live offer with a countdown. Accepting settles like any offer — the token returns to the vendor's inventory and can be re-sold while the vendor is still running. For gacha-style vendors the same prompt appears right after the reveal.
