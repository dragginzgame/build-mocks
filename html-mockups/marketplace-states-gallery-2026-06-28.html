Marketplace — states gallery

The Marketplace: fixed-price browse across all collections. Selecting a collection doesn't change the UI — it just filters the grid to that collection's tokens. Every token uses the approved token card with its rarity border. v1 is Buy Now only — no auctions, no offers. Shared page chrome (context-bar toolbar; the site top bar and footer are their own components). Source: Design Documentation/secondary-marketplace-design.md.

Related: Token detail (buy) · List for sale · Launchpad
01Global browse — all collectionsA top-listings carousel highlights promoted listings up top (the same hero carousel used on the Launchpad and landing page). The grid's default order is by referral fee, highest first — sellers set a 1–10% referral fee when they list, and a higher fee buys higher placement (and feeds the front-page spotlight). There is no separate "Featured" tier. A filter rail (collection, price, issue range, attributes — the rarity-tier filter appears only when a single weighted-rarity collection is selected) sits beside the approved-token-card grid; the white list bar paginates at the bottom. Each card shows collection, name, price + Buy. v1 is fixed-price (Buy Now) only — auctions and offers are deferred.

Nothing listed yet

The marketplace fills up as collectors list what they've claimed. While it warms up, here's how trading works — no surprises, ever.

1

Peer to peer, your terms

Every listing is collector-to-collector and non-custodial: you set the price, the currency, and how long it runs (1–14 days). The token stays in your wallet until the moment it sells.

2

You set the market fee

Sellers choose the marketplace fee — a 1–10% slider. A higher fee earns higher placement in the feed; it's the only visibility lever, and it's only ever charged on a completed sale.

3

The seller pays, not you

Buyers pay one price — nothing is added on top. The project's royalty split and the marketplace fee both come out of that price before the seller's share.

4

Change your mind? Cancel

An unsold listing can be cancelled at any time, and simply returns to your wallet when it expires. No sale, no fee — an unsold listing never costs anything.

Where one sale goes

Every completed sale settles in this order, atomically — the buyer's price is the whole pie. Segment widths illustrative.
